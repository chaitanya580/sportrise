# SportRise — Pitch Deck & Talk Track

For incubator and investor pitches (pre-seed stage).
Editable deck: https://gamma.app/generations/U48f1pRFuuHLQvlL4LG11 (PPTX export available from Gamma).

Target length: 5–6 minutes (30–40 seconds per slide), then Q&A.
Golden rule: **never read the slide.** The slide shows data; you tell the story.

---

## Slide 1 — Title (10 seconds)

**Say:**
> "Hi, I'm Krishna. I'm building SportRise — the platform that gets India's school athletes discovered on merit, not luck. It's live at sportrise.in — you can open it right now."

**Delivery:** The "open it right now" line does the work. A live product in the first ten seconds separates you from everyone pitching an idea.

---

## Slide 2 — Problem

**Say:**
> "Quick question — how did Messi get discovered? A scout happened to watch him play. Now: India has 47 crore active athletes, and less than 0.2% have any digital record of their performance. If you're a talented 13-year-old in Warangal, no scout is coming. Your career depends on who your father knows. Most of that talent quits by sixteen — not for lack of skill, but for lack of visibility."

**Delivery:** Open with the question and pause two seconds. Rooms remember questions, not statements. The Warangal detail makes it concrete — abstract problems don't raise money, specific kids do.

---

## Slide 3 — Solution

**Say:**
> "SportRise makes performance itself the credential. Every coaching session, every match, every tournament win earns XP on an athlete's profile — like a CIBIL score for sporting talent. Athletes climb from Rookie to National Prospect, and National Prospects become searchable by scouts across India. We turn 'who you know' into 'what you've done.'"

**Delivery:** "CIBIL score for sporting talent" is the money line — every Indian investor instantly gets it. Say it slowly.

---

## Slide 4 — Product

**Say:**
> "And this isn't a concept — it's built. Full app: registration, verified coach discovery, one-tap tournament entry, the XP engine, and a scout dashboard. One detail I want to highlight: we built mandatory parental consent for under-18s, enforced at the database level. We're dealing with minors — we took that seriously before anyone made us."

**Delivery:** The compliance point is the maturity signal. Incubators see hundreds of student founders; almost none think about child-safety before launch. Watch for the nod.

---

## Slide 5 — Market

**Say:**
> "Indian sportstech is a nineteen-billion-dollar market, and government momentum through Khelo India is pushing grassroots talent identification harder every year. We're not boiling the ocean — we start with the two sports where parents already spend: football and cricket, in one city, Hyderabad, this September."

**Delivery:** Investors fear "everything for everyone." The phrase "we're not boiling the ocean" preempts their biggest objection before they raise it.

---

## Slide 6 — Business Model

**Say:**
> "We monetize like every great marketplace — a cut of the transactions we create: commission on coaching sessions, fees from tournament organizers, and subscriptions from scouts who want access to the ranked National Prospect database. That database is the asset — nobody else has verified youth performance data. And it's always free for the athlete."

**Delivery:** End on "free for the athlete" — it answers the ethics question about monetizing kids before anyone asks it.

---

## Slide 7 — Go-To-Market

**Say:**
> "Marketplaces die from empty shelves, so we go supply-first: fifty founding coaches in Hyderabad, zero commission for year one. Then schools — free digital sports profiles for every student athlete. And growth is built into the product: athletes earn XP for bringing teammates. Kids recruit each other; leaderboards do our marketing."

**Delivery:** "Marketplaces die from empty shelves" shows you understand the chicken-and-egg problem — the #1 question anyone asks a marketplace founder. You just answered it unprompted.

---

## Slide 8 — Traction

**Say:**
> "Everything I've shown you, I built solo, at zero cost, while in college: the app, the backend, the live site, the analytics funnel. That's not a limitation — that's the proof. Imagine the execution speed with your mentorship and resources behind it."

**Delivery:** This is the judo move — reframe "solo student founder" from weakness into evidence of capital efficiency. Make eye contact here; this slide is really about YOU.

---

## Slide 9 — Competition & Roadmap

**Say:**
> "Academies are offline and opaque. Listing sites are just directories — no verification, no performance data. Nobody owns the verified youth performance record, and that's the moat: every match logged makes the database more valuable to scouts, and every scout makes the platform more valuable to athletes. It compounds. Hyderabad first, then the same playbook in Bengaluru, Pune, Chennai — and the model travels anywhere young talent outstrips scouting infrastructure."

**Delivery:** Say "it compounds" and stop for a beat. Network effects are what investors are actually shopping for.

---

## Slide 10 — Ask

**Say:**
> "I'm asking for two things: admission into your program — I need mentorship on marketplace go-to-market and your connects into Hyderabad's sports ecosystem — and pre-seed capital to fund the pilot: SMS infrastructure, tournament operations, and coach onboarding. The product is built. The city is chosen. Help me put the first thousand athletes on it."

**Delivery:** End on the concrete image — "the first thousand athletes" — not on "thank you." Then stop talking. The silence after a confident ask is where the questions (and the interest) begin.

## Room-Level Tactics

1. **Rehearse slides 2→3 until seamless** — problem-to-solution is the emotional core; if that lands, the rest is detail.
2. **When someone opens sportrise.in on their phone mid-pitch, pause and let them.** A room looking at your live product is winning, not an interruption.
3. **Know your three killer answers cold:**
   - *"How many on the waitlist?"* — Give the real number and the growth plan. Honesty at pre-seed builds more trust than inflated stats.
   - *"Why won't a big academy chain just build this?"* — They profit from opacity; we profit from transparency. Their business model is the reason the problem exists.
   - *"Why you?"* — I'm the age of my users' older brother, I've built the entire product myself, and I'll outwork anyone in this market.

## Honesty Guardrails

- Never claim specific waitlist numbers unless they're real — reviewers probe metrics.
- The app is built but not launched: say "building/built," not "launched." "Launched the landing page / waitlist funnel" is fully accurate.
- Stats used ($19B market, 47Cr athletes, 0.16% digital records) match the live landing page — keep deck and site consistent.
